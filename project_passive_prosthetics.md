## Overview
This project presents a **semi‑active ankle–foot prosthesis** that uses a **pneumatic–hydraulic hybrid mechanism** to modulate ankle stiffness and energy return timing during walking. The design aims to replicate biological ankle behavior by providing:

- Low stiffness during early stance  
- High stiffness and energy storage during mid‑stance  
- Rapid energy release during push‑off  

The system improves gait symmetry, reduces metabolic cost, and enhances user comfort compared to passive prostheses.

The prosthesis integrates:

- A **pneumatic spring** for energy storage  
- A **hydraulic damper** for controlled stiffness modulation  
- A **timing valve system** for stance‑phase switching  
- Embedded sensors for gait‑phase detection  
- A lightweight, low‑power semi‑active control strategy  

The final prototype demonstrated **improved push‑off power**, **more natural ankle kinematics**, and **reduced user effort** during walking trials.

---

## System Summary

### **Hybrid Mechanical Design**
- Pneumatic chamber stores energy during mid‑stance  
- Hydraulic damper regulates stiffness and prevents excessive motion  
- Timing valve switches between “low stiffness” and “high stiffness” modes  
- Designed to mimic biological ankle torque–angle behavior  

### **Control Strategy**
- Gait‑phase detection using IMU + pressure sensors  
- Semi‑active stiffness modulation  
- Push‑off timing optimized for energy return  
- No external power required for actuation (only valves + sensing)

### **Experimental Validation**
- Benchtop stiffness characterization  
- Hysteresis and energy‑storage testing  
- Human‑in‑the‑loop treadmill walking trials  
- Comparison against passive prosthesis baselines  

---
### Key Findings
- Hybrid pneumatic–hydraulic design enables **biologically inspired stiffness modulation**  
- Push‑off timing control improves **energy return efficiency**  
- Semi‑active system requires **minimal power**  
- Demonstrated **more natural gait kinematics**  
- Outperformed passive prosthesis in multiple metrics

## Role

## inovation
The objective of this design initiative is to create a compact, lightweight, and energy-efficient active ankle prosthesis that dynamically adjusts ankle joint stiffness and optimizes energy return while walking. Our innovative pneumatic and hydraulic hybrid system leverages the incompressible characteristics of fluid to effectively store and release energy for push-off support, while utilizing the compressible nature of air to provide variable stiffness for the user. This approach presents several significant advantages: **1) Rapid Response**: The mechanism facilitates quick stiffness modulation and provides additional energy within milliseconds, maintaining a compact and lightweight design. This design strategy effectively minimizes the energy consumption of the prosthesis while facilitating various movement transitions between different activities, thereby offering significant benefits for prosthesis users in real-life situations. **2) Energy Efficient**: The prosthesis is designed to be energy efficient, utilizing a hydraulic mechanism that effectively captures the substantial elastic energy of the carbon fiber blade through simple operation of a valve, either opening or closing it as needed. The proposed design effectively streamlines the system by removing unnecessary power sources and control mechanisms for energy return timing. This optimization leads to a reduction in overall size, decreased battery requirements, and minimized energy consumption, thereby enhancing the potential for extended use. **3) Modular and Versatile Design**: The device features a modular design that facilitates easy integration with various stiffness of prosthetic foot blades, allowing for quick exchanges between carbon fiber blades to achieve personalized stiffness adjustments.
